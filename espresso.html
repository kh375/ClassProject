Espresso

Espresso is coffee of Italian origin, brewed by forcing a small amount of nearly boiling water under pressure through finely ground coffee beans. Loaded with caffeine, this concentrated form of coffee has been used to make various other caffeinated beverages of different styles.

Latte

A latte, also known as “caffè latte”, is typically composed of one shot (1 fl oz) of espresso, 7 fl oz of steamed milk and approximately ½ inch of foamed milk on the top. Various flavors such as vanilla or caramel can be added to make this smooth beverage a sweet treat!

Cappuccino

Cappuccinos are prepared with equal amounts of espresso of hot milk, typically about 2 fl oz each, topped with a thick layer of microfoam on the top. Though cappuccinos are similar to lattes in ingredients, their special mark is the extra layer of foam, which can be topped with cinnamon or cocoa powder for an extra punch.

Americano

An Americano is simply made of espresso, diluted by hot water, giving it a similar strength but different flavor to regular coffee. The term Italiano is used for a stronger version of the Americano, specifically a 1:1 ratio of espresso to water.
